Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to some common questions about the Law Students’ Society.

Membership is open to every duly registered Law student of Joseph Ayo Babalola University. You can obtain and submit the membership form through the LSS Secretariat or download it from the Resources page and follow the instructions provided.
Yes, members may be required to pay dues as approved by the Society in line with the Constitution. Dues help support academic programmes, moots, welfare initiatives and other activities for the benefit of all Law students.
The Society occasionally calls for volunteers to join various committees. Watch the LSS notice boards, WhatsApp platforms and social media pages for announcements, or contact the Executive Council through the form above.
Certain events, such as public lectures and legal awareness programmes, are open to the wider university community. Other activities, like moots or internal training sessions, may be restricted to Law students. Details will usually be stated in the event publicity.
Potential partners, firms or organisations can reach the Society through the official email or by writing to the President of the Law Students’ Society via the Faculty of Law, Joseph Ayo Babalola University.
